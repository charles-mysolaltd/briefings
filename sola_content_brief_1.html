Why content matters now

The gap to close before launch

The core problem: When someone types "can I use plug-in solar in a rented flat UK" into Google, ChatGPT or Perplexity, a competitor blog or YouTube video answers them. Not Sola. These queries are happening thousands of times a month and represent some of your highest-intent prospects: already curious, already price-sensitive, already looking for permission to buy. A small set of tightly focused pages, published before launch, will capture that demand and turn AI assistants into an unpaid sales channel.

Article 01 — Highest priority
Plug-in solar panels UK: is it legal, and do you need permission?
sola.co.uk/blog/plug-in-solar-uk-legal
Legality Renters G98 ~1,600 words

Why this article: "Plug-in solar UK legal" and "balcony solar UK permission" are among the highest-converting queries in this category. Anyone typing this is close to buying. They just need reassurance. The article's job is to remove the last objection before the reader has even reached the product page.

Target queries:

  • "are plug-in solar panels legal in the UK"
  • "do I need permission for plug-in solar UK"
  • "can renters use plug-in solar UK"
  • "G98 notification UK plug-in solar"

Structure:

  • H1: Plug-in solar panels UK: is it legal?
  • Section 1: Yes, plug-in solar is legal in the UK. The rule in plain English: G98 standard, under 3.68kW, DNO notification required.
  • Section 2: Do renters need landlord permission? The honest answer is that plugging into a standard socket is legally equivalent to plugging in a TV. Permanently fixed panels are a different question. Reference the Renters Rights Act 2025.
  • Section 3: What is G98 and do you actually need to notify your DNO? Keep this short and reassuring. Link to Sola's G98 notification tool.
  • Section 4: What about buildings insurance and your lease? Address the common worries clearly.
  • Section 5: The verdict: what most plug-in solar users in the UK actually need to do (very little).
  • CTA: "Ready to get started? See the Sola 400, UK certified, ready to plug in."

Key point: Embed Sola's G98 notification tool on this page. It gives readers a practical reason to visit, return and share, positioning Sola as the authoritative destination for this topic, not just a shop that sells panels.

Article 03 — High priority
Plug-in solar vs rooftop solar: which is right for you?
sola.co.uk/blog/plug-in-solar-vs-rooftop-solar-uk
Comparison Buyers Guide ~1,500 words

Why this article: Comparison queries are among the highest-converting in SEO. Someone searching "plug-in vs rooftop solar" has already decided they want solar and is now deciding how to get it. A genuinely balanced comparison earns more trust than a one-sided one, and AI chatbots prefer balanced sources, because their users notice when a cited piece is clearly written to sell something.

Target queries:

  • "plug-in solar vs rooftop solar UK"
  • "balcony solar worth it UK"
  • "is plug-in solar as good as rooftop"
  • "cheapest way to get solar panels UK"

Structure:

  • H1: Plug-in solar vs rooftop solar: an honest comparison for UK households
  • Section 1: Side-by-side comparison table across cost, installation, annual output, portability, planning requirements and suitability.
  • Section 2: When rooftop solar is the better choice: homeowners with a south-facing roof, a long time horizon and between £6,000 and £8,000 available.
  • Section 3: When plug-in solar is the better choice: renters, recent movers and people who want to start generating this weekend rather than in six months.
  • Section 4: The honest output difference. A rooftop 3.5kW system generates roughly 3,000kWh per year. A Sola 400 generates roughly 300 to 400 kWh; plug-in solar offsets around 10 to 15% of a typical UK household's usage. Say this clearly.
  • Section 5: Can you combine both? Plug-in as a starting point, rooftop later.
  • CTA: "Not ready for rooftop? Start with Sola this weekend. No scaffolding, no waiting list."

Important: Be genuinely fair to rooftop solar. Readers trust balanced comparisons. An article that pretends plug-in wins on every measure will not rank well and will not be cited. Acknowledge the output difference honestly and let the other advantages (cost, portability, accessibility for renters) carry the argument.

Article 04 — Medium priority
How to calculate your solar savings in the UK (with free calculator)
sola.co.uk/blog/calculate-solar-savings-uk
Tool ROI Evergreen ~1,400 words

Why this article: "Solar savings calculator UK" is a perennial query. An article plus embedded calculator creates a link-worthy resource that attracts inbound links from personal finance and energy blogs over time. Calculators get shared; static articles get forgotten. This one is worth building properly.

Target queries:

  • "solar savings calculator UK"
  • "how much can solar panels save me UK"
  • "solar panel payback period calculator UK"
  • "plug-in solar return on investment UK"

Structure:

  • H1: How much can solar panels save you in the UK? (Free calculator)
  • Section 1: What determines your savings? Four variables: panel wattage, location, unit tariff and when you use electricity during the day.
  • Section 2: The embedded calculator. Inputs: panel wattage, region, current unit rate. Outputs: estimated annual kWh generated, annual saving in pounds, payback period. A postcode-to-region lookup is straightforward to build; the rest is multiplication.
  • Section 3: Real examples for UK locations: Manchester, Bristol, London and Edinburgh, with honest winter-versus-summer splits rather than optimistic annual averages.
  • Section 4: How to get the most out of your panel. South-facing orientation, shade avoidance and running high-draw appliances during peak generation hours.
  • Section 5: What the numbers mean for a renter on a budget. Make this human and specific.
  • CTA: "See how the Sola 400 performs in your area."

Build note: The calculator should be a simple JavaScript widget embedded in the page. Postcode-to-region lookup, multiply by standard peak sun hours for that region, apply a 0.8 real-world efficiency factor. This is a two-hour development task, not a complex piece of engineering.

Press strategy

Publications and writers worth targeting

Press coverage does three things simultaneously: it builds high-authority SEO backlinks, it creates trust signals that AI chatbots treat the same way Google treated links in 2005 and it gives Sola credibility with consumers who haven't heard of you. One Which? mention will do more for launch than £5,000 of paid social. Here is where to focus.

Pitch principle: You are not pitching Sola. You are pitching a story. The best ones are "Why can't UK renters access clean energy?" and "The EU already solved this; the UK is three years behind." These are genuinely interesting editorial angles that happen to feature your product. Journalists write about stories, not products.

Outreach sequence: Build a one-page press kit first: product images, key specs, founder background, two or three data points. Identify two or three journalists from each Tier 1 publication who have written about energy, renters or clean technology in the past six months. Write a personalised email for each one that references their previous work and explains why this is a story for their readers specifically. Offer a kit for testing. Follow up once, seven days later. Don't chase further. Run Tier 2 outreach in parallel. Use every piece of coverage immediately as social proof on the website and in email.
What to avoid: Don't send a generic press release to a bulk list. "Sola launches new solar product" is not a story. Don't pay for advertorial coverage on low-credibility sites. AI chatbots can tell the difference and so can readers. One earned mention in Which? is worth more than fifty paid placements elsewhere.
Video content — Synthesia production plan

7 explainer videos: purpose, placement and script outlines

These videos are designed for Synthesia, using an AI presenter. No filming, no studio. Each runs 90 to 180 seconds, embeds on the relevant article or product page and goes to YouTube as a standalone video. The 30-second cut goes to TikTok and Instagram Reels. One three-minute Synthesia video costs nothing to produce and generates four pieces of content across three platforms. Scripts should be tight, simple and direct. The presenter should speak to someone who has never thought about solar before.

Platform strategy: Upload every video to YouTube with a keyword-rich title and description. YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine and Google increasingly surfaces video results above blog posts for "how to" queries. Embed each video on the corresponding Sola page. Cut 30-second clips for TikTok and Reels. The YouTube channel also acts as a long-term SEO asset; videos from 2026 will still be generating views in 2029.

Do plug-in solar panels work in winter and on cloudy days in the UK?
