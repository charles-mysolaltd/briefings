Pricing & margins

Revised pricing across all three kits

Current pricing: £199 / £299 / £599 upfront across the three kits. At 50-unit launch volumes the Sola 400 lands at roughly 38–42% gross margin on upfront sales. That is tight but workable given lean fixed costs. The Klarna path (£396 total over 18 months) gets you to 50%+ on the same unit, since Klarna pays within 48 hours. The target is to reach 50% gross margin on upfront sales by Order 2, when landed COGS on the 400W drops to around £150.

On the ROI framing at £22/month: The Hero 400W saves £10–13/month on bills. Net monthly cost to the customer is roughly £9–12. The honest framing is: spread the cost, own it outright after 18 months, then save for free. At £299 upfront, payback sits at 1.9–2.5 years, which is a genuinely strong number to lead with for upfront buyers.
On month-1 cash-positivity: There is no price point at which this product is net-positive from month 1 without subsidising it or waiting for COGS to fall at scale. The right move is not to promise month-1 ROI. Say instead: lowest monthly cost of any kit on the market, own it outright in 18 months, saving for 10+ years after that.

Total addressable market

Who can actually use Sola?

The UK market is much larger than just balcony owners. The full TAM includes renters with gardens, homeowners wanting a portable/supplemental system, and even boat/caravan owners. Here's the breakdown.
Private renter households
4.6M+

UK homes with gardens
~21M

UK flats (leasehold)
~4.98M
70% of all leasehold homes are flats. Many have balconies or accessible outdoor space. These are your highest-concentration urban customers.

Shipping & delivery

Shipping costs by kit

Panel dimensions and weight vary significantly — the 800W Pro ships as two large panels (172cm × 113cm × 3.5cm, 21.8kg each) and may require a pallet or oversized courier. The 200 and 400 compact panels are standard parcel territory. Your 3PL handles this automatically but you need to price delivery accurately on Shopify.

Strategy for 200 and 400: Offer free standard delivery. This removes friction at checkout and is the norm for electronics. Build the £9–13 shipping cost into your COGS. Charge for next day (£10–13 to the customer, costs you £13–18, so small subsidy or break-even). Most customers will choose free standard.
Strategy for 800: Two rigid 172cm panels make this an oversized shipment you cannot absorb. Charge £14.99 standard (costs you ~£25–35 via 3PL, so a subsidy of roughly £10–20). The cleaner option: offer free delivery when the customer chooses Klarna, where your margin is higher anyway.
Important: The 800W Pro panels (172cm × 113cm each) may exceed standard courier limits. DHL handles up to 120cm × 70cm × 70cm. At 172cm length these will likely require Parcelforce 48 (up to 1.5m) or a specialist freight carrier. Your 3PL will handle routing, but flag this when onboarding them and ask specifically about "long/large" handling and fragile electronics surcharges. Budget £30–45 per 800W delivery at first.

The DTC channel is the launch channel, not the only channel. A reseller strategy diversifies revenue, reduces dependence on paid social and reduces customer acquisition cost. The common thread across every channel below is organisations that already have a trusted relationship with your end customer and a reason to offer an energy-saving product. One BTR developer piloting Sola across 50 balconies is worth more than 50 individual DTC sales in terms of volume, logistics simplicity and proof of concept for investors.

Trade pricing model
25–35% off RRP
A trade price of roughly £195–225 on the Sola 400 (RRP £299) still leaves 15–25% gross margin on B2B sales at 100-unit COGS. Volume per account offsets the margin compression. Do not discount below £190 without a minimum order commitment in writing.
B2B Klarna note
Cash on invoice
B2B buyers pay on invoice (30-day terms standard). You will not use Klarna for trade sales. This means you carry the stock risk until payment. Mitigate with 50% deposit on first orders from any new trade account, full payment thereafter on 30-day terms.
Customer acquisition

Fintech partnership programmes

The core idea: fintech loyalty programmes let Sola fund a small reward (RevPoints, cashback) on each purchase, in exchange for being listed and surfaced to the platform's user base. The customer perceives meaningful value; the actual cost to Sola is sub-2% at modest reward levels. This makes it one of the cheapest customer acquisition channels available, provided the demographic fit is right. Revolut's 10 million UK users skew heavily towards digitally-native, financially-aware 25–40 year olds in cities — the core Sola persona.

How Revolut Merchant Rewards works

Revolut's Merchant Rewards programme allows any Revolut Business merchant to offer boosted RevPoints to customers who pay via Revolut Pay. There is no fee to launch a campaign or to be listed in the Shops tab. Sola pays only on successful transactions: a per-transaction fee made up of the reward amount funded by Sola plus Revolut's platform fee, deducted before the net sale proceeds are credited to the Revolut Business account.

No setup or listing fee
Performance only
Sola pays nothing unless a customer buys via Revolut Pay with the reward active. No minimum spend commitment, no monthly retainer.
Revolut Pay requirement
Already covered
Merchant Rewards is only available on Revolut Pay transactions. Sola is integrating Revolut Pay on Shopify, so this prerequisite is already being satisfied.

Packaging strategy

On-box vs. leaflet instructions

Recommendation: hybrid approach. Print the 5-step install guide on the box (as shown in the packaging designs), but also include one thin A5 folded card inside with:

1) The landlord permission template letter (pre-written, customer just signs)
2) The Sola app QR code and Wi-Fi pairing instructions
3) Warranty registration card
Why on-box instructions win: Customers frequently throw away instruction leaflets before reading them. If the 5 steps are on the box itself, the instructions are always present before, during and after unboxing. This is what Apple does with AirPods: the packaging is the instruction manual. It also signals confidence in the product's simplicity.
📦

Unboxing experience strategy

The box opens from the top. Panel(s) visible immediately in protective foam. Inverter and cable in a branded tray underneath. One card on top with just three words: "Plug in. Generate. Save." Everything else is on the box exterior.

♻️

Sustainable packaging signal

Use recycled card (FSC certified). Print "100% recycled packaging" on the box — this resonates with your eco audience and costs ~£1–2 extra per unit. The box should be strong enough to reuse for storage — don't make it feel disposable.

🎁

Gift-worthy design

Father's Day and Christmas require a gift-ready box. The amber/teal/dark colourway already looks premium. Add "Gift wrapping available" at checkout (£3.99 extra — free money, zero effort for 3PL).
